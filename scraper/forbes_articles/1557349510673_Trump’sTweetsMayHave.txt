Topline: United States President Donald Trump may have helped trigger a 150% spike in Ohio-based electric vehicle maker Workhorse’s stocks after he tweeted that General Motors would be selling its shuttered Lordstown car factory to the group, further evidence of the president’s twitter feed’s influence on markets.   In the past, President Trump’s tweets have driven other companies’ stock prices up and down during a trading day, according to a study by Northern University. And in February, oil markets fell in reaction to President Trump's tweet about oil prices rising too high.
