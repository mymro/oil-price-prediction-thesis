The United States conducted at least $1 billion in two-way trade with 94 countries in 2017, more than one-third of the world's nations. It conducted at least $10 billion with the top 45, $50 billion with the top 17, more than $100 billion with the top seven, and $500 billion with the top three. What follows is a look at the nation's top 10 trade partners in 2017, with links to more detailed information. All the information is based on annual U.S. Census Bureau, as analyzed by the WorldCity, the company where I serve as president. This post follows one on the top 10 ranked airports, seaports and border crossings for U.S. trade. Still to come are columns on the top 10 U.S. exports and the top 10 imports.
