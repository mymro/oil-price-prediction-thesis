Chevron (NYSE: CVX) is a multinational, oil company based out of San Ramon, California. It is active in over 180 countries and employs over 48,600 people across its operations. The company provides a range of Upstream and Downstream services and competes with Exxon, Royal Dutch Shell, and British Petroleum. Trefis highlights trends in Chevron’s Revenue over the years, and expects the figure to decline in 2019 due to its sensitivity to commodity prices. While Chevron is expected to increase both its crude and natural gas production for the year, a weak macro environment and the resulting lower prices for these commodities are weighing on the company’s top line. Additionally, Chevron has been forced to write-down $11-billion worth of natural gas assets at a time when oil companies have been picking up smaller natural gas players – indicating of near term headwinds for the industry as a whole.
