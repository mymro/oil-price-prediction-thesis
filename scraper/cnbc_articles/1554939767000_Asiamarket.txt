The Shenzhen composite also fell 2.187 percent to 1,740.37.Hong Kong's Hang Seng index shed 0.81 percent in its final hour of trading. Chinese tech giant Tencent's stock rose more than 1 percent, earlier crossing the 400 Hong Kong dollar per share mark for the first time since June 2018.The moves in China came after the country's consumer inflation in March touched a 5-month high on the back of higher food prices."We continue to forecast CPI will be 2.1% in 2019.
