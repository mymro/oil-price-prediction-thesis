March 12 (Reuters) - Futures pointed to a higher opening for Canada’s main stock index on Tuesday, tracking gains in oil prices. Saudi Arabia’s plan for further voluntary supply curbs in April and a cut in oil exports from Venezuela due to a power outage supported oil prices. March futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.17 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX ended 110.03 points, or 0.69 percent, higher to 16,106.24 on Monday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were down 0.2 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were down 0.01 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.09 percent.
