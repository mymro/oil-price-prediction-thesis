Feb 4 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index opened higher on Tuesday as a rebound in oil prices on hopes of additional production cuts from OPEC, powered an over 2% jump in energy shares. * At 09:30 a.m. ET (14:32 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was up 122.82 points, or 0.71%, at 17,502.58.
