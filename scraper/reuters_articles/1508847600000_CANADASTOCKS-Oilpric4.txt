Oct 24 (Reuters) - Stock futures for Canada’s main stock index pointed to a higher opening on Tuesday as oil prices rose. Crude prices found support from supply disruptions in Iraq and after Saudi Arabia said it is determined to reduce crude inventories further. December futures on the S&P TSX index were up 0.16 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index ended little changed on Monday, as a plunge in Eldorado Gold Corp and losses for energy stocks offset gains in the financial and mining sectors. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.25 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.09 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.11 percent.
