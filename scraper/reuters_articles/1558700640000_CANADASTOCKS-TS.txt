May 24 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main equity index rose on Friday, as oil prices rose after two sessions of losses. However, crude prices remained on track for its biggest weekly drop this year due to rising inventories and concerns of a global economic slowdown. June futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.43% at 7:00 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX fell 162.74 points, or 1%, to 16,164.61 on Thursday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.67% at 7:00 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.65% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.58%.
