Feb 12 (Reuters) - Stock futures pointed to a higher opening for Canada’s main stock index on Monday, as oil prices rose after a week of steep losses. March futures on the S&P TSX index were up 0.68 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index fell on Friday, closing at its lowest level in five months, reflecting weakness in mining stocks as gold futures and copper prices declined. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 1.11 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 1.06 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 1.02 percent.
