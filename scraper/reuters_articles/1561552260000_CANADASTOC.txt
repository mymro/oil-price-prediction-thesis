June 26 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index futures rose on Wednesday as oil prices jumped after data showed a decline in U.S. crude inventories and an outage at a major refinery on the U.S. East Coast. September futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.27% at 7:10 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX fell 152.19 points, or 0.92%, to 16,371.28 on Tuesday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.4% at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.43% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.68%. Brent crude futures earlier touched their highest since May 31 at $66.25 a barrel and U.S. crude hit its highest level since May 30 at $59.13 a barrel. South Africa’s Sibanye-Stillwater is to sell a 51% stake in its PGM-copper project in northern Ontario to Generation Mining Ltd in a deal to further develop the asset, the previous miner said on Wednesdaay.
