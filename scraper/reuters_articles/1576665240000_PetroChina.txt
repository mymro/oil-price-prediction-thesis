LONDON (Reuters) - PetroChina International Singapore has offered the lowest price for a liquified natural gas (LNG) cargo in a Pakistan LNG tender for February delivery, a document on the Pakistani company’s website showed on Wednesday. PetroChina’s offer was at 8.59% of Brent crude oil prices. Other offers came from commodity traders Gunvor, Trafigura and SOCAR Trading, with prices ranging from 8.68% to 9.77%. Pakistan LNG was seeking a cargo for delivery on Feb. 16-17 2020.
