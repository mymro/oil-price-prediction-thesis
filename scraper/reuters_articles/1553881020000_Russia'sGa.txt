MOSCOW (Reuters) - Russian gas producers Gazprom and RusGazDobycha will start a major gas processing and liquefaction project near the Baltic Sea port of Ust-Luga, Gazprom said on Friday.   The project will have a capacity of 13 million tonnes of liquefied natural gas (LNG), while investment in the project will total 700 billion roubles  ($10.67 billion), it said.  The first block of the complex is to become operational in the second half of 2023 and the second block by the end of 2024, Gazprom said.
