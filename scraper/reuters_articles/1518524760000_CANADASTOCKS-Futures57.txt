Feb 13 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index were little changed on Tuesday, as oil prices edged lower after the International Energy Agency estimated world crude supply could overtake demand this year. March futures on the S&P TSX index were up 0.02 percent at 7:20 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index recovered on Monday to post its biggest one-day gain since July 2016, with all 10 sectors in positive territory, taking their cue from a rebound in global stocks, oil and metals following a brutal week. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were down 0.54 percent at 7:20 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were down 0.47 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were down 0.53 percent.
