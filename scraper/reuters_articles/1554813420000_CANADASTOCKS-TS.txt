April 9 (Reuters) - Stock futures pointed to a lower opening for Canada’s main stock index on Tuesday after a six-day run of gains. Oil hovered near five-month highs on Tuesday, supported by concerns that violence in Libya could further tighten supply, although Russian comments signalling willingness to pump more dampened the rally. June futures on the S&P/TSX index were down 0.04 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX closed up 11.14 points, or 0.07 percent, at 16,407.29 on Monday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were down 0.03 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were down 0.09 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were down 0.12 percent. Burger King, a subsidiary of Toronto-listed Restaurant Brands, pulled a promotional video in New Zealand showing customers trying to eat burgers with chopsticks after it sparked an outcry in China and demands for an apology from the U.S. fast food chain.
