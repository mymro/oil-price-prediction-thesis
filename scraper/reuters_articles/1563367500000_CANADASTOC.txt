July 17 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index futures edged up on Wednesday, as oil prices rose even after data showed U.S. crude inventories fell less than expected. Last week, U.S. crude inventories fell less than expected, gasoline too decreased, while distillate rose, industry group the American Petroleum Institute said on Tuesday. September futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.1% at 7:00 a.m. ET. Canada’s inflation and factory sales data are due at 8:30 a.m. ET The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX fell 8.40 points, or 0.05%, to 16,502.42 on Tuesday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.05% at 7:00 a.m. ET, S&P 500 e-mini futures 0.04% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures 0.13%.
