Sept 9 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index edged up on Monday, following a rise in oil prices after Saudi Arabia’s new energy minister confirmed his country’s commitment to OPEC-led crude supply cuts. Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman, who took over as energy minister from Khalid al-Falih on Sunday, said on Monday the world’s top oil exporter would keep working with other producers to achieve market balance and that an OPEC-led supply-curbing deal would survive “with the will of everybody”. September futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.05% at 7:00 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange S&P/TSX composite index fell 39.48 points, or 0.24% to 16,535.33 on Friday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.19% at 7:00 a.m. ET, S&P 500 e-mini futures gained 0.23% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures 0.18%.
