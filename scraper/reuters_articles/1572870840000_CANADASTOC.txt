Nov 4 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index rose on Monday, as hopes that the U.S. and China might sign a trade deal later this month lifted oil prices. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index closed up 0.67% at 16,594.07 on Friday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.45% at 7:00 a.m. ET, S&P 500 e-mini futures 0.47% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures 0.58%.
