Dec 28 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index rose on Friday, as oil prices bounced back from the previous session’s steep losses. March futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.72 percent at 7:07 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange S&P/TSX composite index closed up 385.02 points, or 2.79 percent, at 14,165.21 on Thursday. Oil prices rebounded, recovering slightly from heavy losses this week, but remained close to the lowest levels in over a year as rising U.S. inventories and concern over global economic growth rattled markets. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.53 percent at 7:16 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.51 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.4 percent. Canadian cannabis producer Aphria Inc said that Green Growth Brands Ltd’s proposed C$2.8 billion ($2.06 billion) all-stock hostile bid undervalued the company.
