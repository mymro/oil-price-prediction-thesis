Aug 14 (Reuters) - Futures at Canada’s main stock index rose on Tuesday, tracking gains in global markets as Turkish lira recovered from a two-day rout. September futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.38 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index edged higher on Monday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.36 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.36 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.46 percent. Canadian oilfield services provider Ensign Energy Services Inc on Monday said it would make a C$947 million ($720 million) hostile bid for Trinidad Drilling Ltd, as the recovery of Canada’s oil industry lags a global rebound.
