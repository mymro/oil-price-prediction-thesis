Aug 15 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index edged lower on Wednesday as oil prices fell due to a gloomier outlook for global economy and a report of rising U.S. crude inventories. September futures on the S&P/TSX index were down 0.25 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index closed up on Tuesday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were down 0.51 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were down 0.46 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were down 0.65 percent. Constellation Brands Inc said it would invest $4 billion more in Canopy Growth Corp, raising its stake in the Canadian marijuana producer to 38 percent. Air France-KLM’s board is likely to appoint Air Canada’s chief operating officer Benjamin Smith as its new boss on Thursday, according to French newspaper Liberation.
