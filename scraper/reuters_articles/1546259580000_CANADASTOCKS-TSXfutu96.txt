Dec 31 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index were higher on Monday, as oil prices rose on hints of progress on a possible Sino-U.S. trade deal. U.S. President Donald Trump tweeted he had a “long and very good call” with Chinese President Xi Jinping and that a possible trade deal between the two nations was progressing well. March futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.75 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX rose 56.79 points, or 0.40 percent, to 14,222.00 on Friday to cap off a weekly gain of more than 2 percent, the best weekly performance since February. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.94 percent at 7:00 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.91 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 1.06 percent. Large crowds have flocked to Canada Goose’s new outdoor wear store in downtown Beijing, its first in mainland China, since its opening on Friday, despite sub-freezing temperatures and a chill in bilateral ties.
