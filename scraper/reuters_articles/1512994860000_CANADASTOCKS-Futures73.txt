Dec 11 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada’s main stock index were little changed on Monday amid a fall in oil prices on signs of an increase in U.S. drilling activity. December futures on the S&P TSX index were up 0.03 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index rose to a two-week high on Friday, led by financial and industrial shares after solid U.S. jobs data boosted investor sentiment, while energy stocks climbed on higher oil prices. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.17 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.06 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.11 percent.
