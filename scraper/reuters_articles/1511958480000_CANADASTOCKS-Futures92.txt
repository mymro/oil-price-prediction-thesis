Nov 29 (Reuters) - Stock futures pointed to a higher opening for Canada’s main stock index on Wednesday following Royal Bank of Canada’s strong quarterly results. December futures on the S&P TSX index were up 0.09 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index slipped on Tuesday, weighed by a decline in Bank of Nova Scotia shares after the company agreed to buy a bank in Chile, and as investors were rattled after North Korea fired a ballistic missile. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.31 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.12 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.09 percent.
