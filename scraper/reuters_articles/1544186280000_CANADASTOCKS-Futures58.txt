Dec 7 (Reuters) - Stock futures for Canada’s main stock index were little changed on Friday, as investors awaited the outcome of the crucial OPEC meeting and the release of national jobs data later in the day. OPEC talks on oil production cuts reached a deadlock as the group’s leader Saudi Arabia refused to grant sanctions-hit Iran exemptions from planned reductions, OPEC sources said. December futures on the S&P/TSX index were down 0.07 percent at 6:55 a.m. ET. Canada’s jobs data is due at 8:30 a.m. ET. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX fell 245.64 points, or 1.62 percent, to 14,937.00 on Thursday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were down 0.5 percent at 6:55 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were down 0.6 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were down 0.7 percent. • Capital Power Corp: RBC raises price target to C$29 from C$28. • Dollarama Inc: CIBC cuts target price to C$37 from C$40.
