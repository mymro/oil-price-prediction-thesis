Sept 3 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index opened lower on Tuesday, as higher OPEC and Russian oil output weighed on prices, pressuring energy shares. * At 9:30 a.m. ET (13:30 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was down 59.54 points, or 0.36%, at 16,382.53.
