LONDON, March 15 (Reuters) - The world’s largest oil trader Vitol has appointed Russell Hardy as group chief executive, the company said on Thursday, while long-time CEO Ian Taylor has become chairman. Vitol said in a statement that Hardy joined the firm in 1993 from BP and held a number of trading and management roles in Singapore and London. He has been a member of Vitol’s executive committee since 2007 and was named CEO of the Europe, Middle-East and Africa region in 2017. (Reporting By Julia Payne; Editing by Susan Fenton)
