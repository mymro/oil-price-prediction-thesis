Jan 10 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index retreated from all-time highs hit in the previous session on Friday, as a decline in oil prices on easing tensions in the Middle East pressured energy stocks. * At 09:37 a.m. ET (14:37 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was down 29.59 points, or 0.17%, at 17,205.98.
