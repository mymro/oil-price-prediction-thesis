Oct 26 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index opened lower on Friday, taking a cue from global markets and a drop in crude oil prices. * At 9:30 a.m. ET (13:30 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was down 94.13 points, or 0.63 percent, at 14,829.95.
