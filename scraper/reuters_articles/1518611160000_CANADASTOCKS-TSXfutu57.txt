Feb 14 (Reuters) - Futures pointed to a lower opening for Canada’s main stock index on Wednesday, as oil prices fell pressured by rising U.S. crude production. March futures on the S&P TSX index were down 0.23 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET. The Thomson Reuters/Ipsos Primary Consumer Sentiment Index is due at 11:00 a.m. ET. Canada’s main stock index fell modestly on Tuesday, pausing after racking up a hefty gain in the previous session, as industrial and energy shares fell along with the price of oil. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.45 percent at 7:15 a.m. ET, while S&P 500 e-mini futures were up 0.44 percent and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures were up 0.46 percent.
