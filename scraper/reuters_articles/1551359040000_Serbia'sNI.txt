BELGRADE, Feb 28 (Reuters) - Serbian oil company NIS , majority owned by Russia’s Gazprom Neft, reported on Thursday a 7 percent fall in 2018 net profit to 25.1 billion Serbian dinars ($242.70 million) due to a rise in costs. It said earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortisation (EBITDA) rose 14 percent year-on-year to 53.7 billion dinars. The company produced 1.3 million tonnes of oil and gas equivalent in the period, down from 1.4 million tonnes a year before, it said. Total crude oil and semi-finished product refining volume hit a record 3.8 million tonnes, up from 3.6 million tonnes in 2017. Investment in exploration, production and capacity modernisation rose 55 percent to 41 billion dinars, it said. One of the biggest contributors to Serbia’s budget, NIS operates two refineries, plus fields in Serbia, Angola and Bosnia. Russia’s Gazprom Neft owns 56.15 percent of NIS, while the Serbian government owns 29.88 percent. NIS shares rose 3.57 percent to 725 dinars on Thursday.
