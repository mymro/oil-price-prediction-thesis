Feb 12 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index opened higher on Tuesday, lifted by energy stocks as oil prices surged on OPEC-led production cuts. * At 9:30 a.m. ET (14:30 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was up 44.27 points, or 0.28 percent, at 15,613.12.
