June 10 (Reuters) - Stock futures for Canada’s main stock index were little changed on Monday as oil prices steadied, with Saudi Arabia and Russia yet to agree on an output-cutting deal. June futures on the S&P/TSX index were down 0.01% at 7:00 a.m. ET. Canada’s housing starts and building permits data are due at 8:15 a.m. ET and 8:30 a.m. ET, respectively. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX rose 3.16 points, or 0.02%, to 16,230.96 on Friday. Dow Jones Industrial Average e-mini futures were up 0.41% at 7:00 a.m. ET, S&P 500 e-mini futures 0.38% and Nasdaq 100 e-mini futures 0.46%. Finnish engineering firm Metso has agreed to buy Canadian mobile crushing and screening equipment manufacturer McCloskey International for 420 million Canadian dollars to expand its product range and client base.
