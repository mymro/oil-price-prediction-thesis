Dec 17 (Reuters) - Futures pointed to a higher opening for Canada’s main stock index on Monday as oil prices edged up, after slipping nearly 2 percent last week. A Glencore-controlled mining company and some of its current and former executives have agreed to pay more than $22 million to settle Canadian allegations they hid the risks of doing business with an Israeli close to Congolese President Joseph Kabila, the Wall Street Journal reported.
